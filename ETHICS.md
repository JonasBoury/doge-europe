# Research Ethics Guidelines

## Purpose

This document establishes ethical guidelines for using the DOGE Europe research assistant. Investigative research into political corruption and fraud serves the public interest, but must be conducted responsibly and ethically.

## Core Principles

### 1. Public Interest

**Legitimate Uses:**
- Exposing corruption and fraud
- Holding public officials accountable
- Promoting government transparency
- Supporting investigative journalism
- Academic research into political systems
- Civic engagement and informed citizenship

**Prohibited Uses:**
- Personal vendettas or harassment
- Defamation or character assassination
- Political opposition research for partisan gain alone
- Invasion of privacy without public interest justification
- Commercial exploitation of private information
- Doxxing or targeting private individuals

### 2. Truth and Accuracy

**Commitments:**
- Verify all claims with multiple credible sources
- Clearly distinguish between facts, allegations, and opinions
- Correct errors promptly and transparently
- Acknowledge uncertainty and limitations
- Present evidence objectively
- Consider alternative explanations

**Prohibitions:**
- Fabricating or distorting evidence
- Cherry-picking data to fit a narrative
- Presenting speculation as fact
- Ignoring contradictory evidence
- Misleading through selective omission
- Sensationalizing findings

### 3. Privacy and Harm Minimization

**Balancing Test:**
Public interest in disclosure must outweigh:
- Privacy interests of individuals
- Potential harm to innocent parties
- National security concerns (when legitimate)
- Ongoing law enforcement investigations

**Privacy Protections:**
- Minimize exposure of purely private information
- Protect non-public figures absent compelling justification
- Redact unnecessary personal details
- Consider impact on family members
- Respect victims and vulnerable individuals

**Special Protections:**
- Children and minors
- Victims of crimes
- Whistleblowers and confidential sources
- Private citizens peripherally connected to stories
- Medical and financial information

### 4. Source Protection

**Confidentiality:**
- Never reveal confidential sources without permission
- Use secure communications
- Remove identifying information from documents
- Resist legal pressure to disclose sources
- Maintain proper operational security

**Source Rights:**
- Explain how information will be used
- Honor agreements about attribution
- Protect sources from retaliation
- Don't mislead sources about intentions
- Give sources reasonable opportunity to reconsider

### 5. Legal Compliance

**Belgian Law Compliance:**
- Respect Belgian privacy laws (GDPR)
- Follow FOI request procedures properly
- Don't illegally obtain information yourself
- Understand libel and defamation law
- Respect court orders and legal restrictions
- Follow Belgian journalistic codes

**Whistleblower Protection:**
- Understand Belgian whistleblower laws
- Don't encourage illegal activity
- Protect legitimate whistleblowers
- Know legal risks they face

**Received Leaks:**
- May publish legally obtained leaks in public interest
- Verify authenticity thoroughly
- Consider source motivation
- Assess public interest vs. harm
- Consult legal counsel when appropriate

### 6. Independence and Conflicts of Interest

**Maintain Independence:**
- Disclose any conflicts of interest
- Don't accept gifts or favors from subjects
- Avoid financial interests in outcomes
- Resist pressure from powerful interests
- Maintain editorial independence

**Declare Conflicts:**
- Personal relationships with subjects
- Financial interests in outcomes
- Political affiliations affecting objectivity
- Previous employment or connections
- Any factor affecting impartiality

### 7. Right of Reply

**Fair Treatment:**
- Contact subjects for comment before publication
- Give reasonable time to respond
- Fairly present their responses
- Allow correction of factual errors
- Consider their perspective

**Exceptions:**
- When safety requires anonymity
- When subject would destroy evidence
- When delay would harm public interest
- When subject is clearly evading
- When subject has repeatedly refused

### 8. Proportionality

**Appropriate Methods:**
- Use least intrusive methods necessary
- Don't employ deception without compelling justification
- Match investigative intensity to importance
- Consider impact on all parties
- Use public records before seeking leaks

**Deception:**
Generally avoid deception. Use only when:
- Story is of vital public importance
- Information cannot be obtained by other means
- Public interest clearly outweighs deception
- Deception is disclosed in publication

### 9. Respect for Democratic Processes

**Support Democracy:**
- Promote informed citizenship
- Expose anti-democratic behavior
- Respect legitimate privacy of political process
- Distinguish between policy disagreement and corruption

**Not Partisan Warfare:**
- Apply same standards to all political parties
- Don't serve as opposition research arm
- Investigate corruption regardless of perpetrator
- Maintain objectivity and fairness

## Specific Scenarios

### Investigating Public Officials

**Enhanced Scrutiny Justified:**
- Public officials have reduced privacy expectations
- Public interest in their conduct is high
- Their decisions affect the public
- Accountability is essential to democracy

**Still Protected:**
- Purely private family matters
- Medical information
- Unrelated personal life
- Information without public interest nexus

### Investigating Private Individuals

**Higher Bar:**
- Private figures have greater privacy rights
- Require stronger public interest justification
- Focus on actions, not personal life
- Minimize identification when possible

**Justification Required:**
- Direct involvement in public corruption
- Beneficiaries of public corruption
- Gatekeepers enabling corruption
- Public interest outweighs privacy

### Using Leaked Documents

**Assessment Framework:**
1. **Authenticity**: Are documents genuine?
2. **Public Interest**: Does release serve public good?
3. **Harm Assessment**: Who could be harmed?
4. **Proportionality**: Is harm justified by public interest?
5. **Legality**: Could publishing create legal liability?

**Best Practices:**
- Verify thoroughly before publishing
- Consult legal counsel
- Redact unnecessary private information
- Protect source identity
- Consider phased release if appropriate

### International Investigations

**Cross-Border Considerations:**
- Different jurisdictions have different laws
- GDPR applies to Belgian/EU data
- Some countries have oppressive laws
- Source protection varies by country
- Libel tourism is real

## Institutional Safeguards

### Before Publishing/Sharing

**Checklist:**
- [ ] All major facts verified by multiple sources
- [ ] Alternative explanations considered
- [ ] Subjects given opportunity to respond
- [ ] Unnecessary private information redacted
- [ ] Sources protected appropriately
- [ ] Legal review if needed
- [ ] Public interest clearly demonstrated
- [ ] Proportionality assessed
- [ ] Conflicts of interest disclosed
- [ ] Methods were ethical

### If Errors Occur

**Correction Protocol:**
1. Acknowledge error promptly
2. Correct prominently
3. Explain what was wrong
4. Apologize if appropriate
5. Investigate how error occurred
6. Improve processes to prevent recurrence

### If Ethics Concerns Arise

**When Uncertain:**
- Consult with experienced journalists
- Seek legal advice when appropriate
- Consider multiple perspectives
- Err on side of caution
- Document decision-making
- Be prepared to defend choices

## Accountability

### This Project's Commitments

**We Commit To:**
- Following these ethical guidelines
- Prioritizing truth and accuracy
- Respecting privacy and minimizing harm
- Protecting sources
- Maintaining independence
- Correcting errors
- Operating transparently
- Serving the public interest

**We Will Not:**
- Publish unverified allegations
- Serve partisan interests
- Invade privacy without justification
- Reveal confidential sources
- Accept conflicts of interest
- Ignore our ethical obligations

### User Responsibilities

**Users Must:**
- Follow these ethical guidelines
- Use the tool for legitimate purposes
- Verify information before acting on it
- Respect privacy and legal boundaries
- Take responsibility for their use of findings
- Report bugs or ethical concerns
- Not misuse the research capabilities

**Users Must Not:**
- Use for illegal purposes
- Harass or target individuals
- Publish unverified claims
- Violate privacy without justification
- Ignore ethical standards
- Misrepresent findings

## Conclusion

Investigating political corruption serves democracy, but power requires responsibility. These guidelines ensure that our pursuit of accountability respects human dignity, legal boundaries, and democratic values.

When in doubt, ask: Does this serve the public interest? Is it true? Is it fair? Is it necessary? Can we defend our methods?
