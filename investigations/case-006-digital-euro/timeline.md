# Timeline: Digital Euro

## 2020
- **Oct 2020** — ECB publishes "Report on a digital euro," launching the exploration phase

## 2021
- **Jul 2021** — ECB Governing Council decides to launch a two-year investigation phase
- **Oct 2021** — Investigation phase begins
- **Oct 2021** — Nigeria launches eNaira (later widely considered a failure)

## 2022
- **Sep 2022** — ECB selects UI prototyping partners including CaixaBank, Worldline, EPI, Nexi, and Amazon
- **Nov 2022** — ECB engages consultancies: Accenture, BearingPoint, Deloitte, Oliver Wyman, Roland Berger
- **Dec 2022** — Nigeria engineers cash shortage to force eNaira adoption; protests follow

## 2023
- **Jun 2023** — European Commission publishes Digital Euro Regulation proposal (COM/2023/369)
- **Oct 2023** — Investigation phase concludes; ECB Governing Council decides to proceed to preparation phase
- **Nov 2023** — Preparation phase begins (planned through October 2025)
- **Dec 2023** — European Banking Federation publishes study: €739B potential deposit outflow at €3,000 limit

## 2024
- **Jan 2024** — ECB initiates procurement for technology providers
- **Dec 2024** — European Parliament ECON Committee rapporteur changes to Fernando Navarrete Rojas

## 2025
- **Sep 2025** — ECB claims digital euro will be "profit-making" for payment service providers
- **Oct 2025** — ECB completes preparation phase; selects technology providers (framework contracts up to €237M)
- **Oct 2025** — ECB Governing Council decides to move to next phase (building technical capacity)
- **Nov 2025** — Parliament ECON Committee draft report presented
- **Dec 2025** — Council of the EU agrees negotiating position on digital euro regulation
- **Dec 2025** — Amendments deadline for Parliament ECON Committee

## 2026 (Projected)
- **Q1 2026** — Call for expression of interest from payment service providers
- **H1 2026** — Expected European Parliament vote on Digital Euro Regulation (outcome uncertain)
- **Jan 2026** — China makes e-CNY interest-bearing to boost adoption

## 2027 (Projected)
- **H2 2027** — Pilot exercise with payment service providers (12-month duration)

## 2029 (Projected)
- **2029** — Earliest possible first issuance of digital euro (contingent on legislation)
