# Timeline: Digitalization of the Belgian Justice System

## Timeline Framework

This timeline documents key events in the digitalization of Belgium's justice system, from early initiatives to the present day.

---

## Pre-2010: Early Digitalization Attempts

### 2000s
- **Event:** Initial computerization efforts in Belgian courts
- **Details:** Fragmented, department-level initiatives with no central coordination
- **Significance:** Set the pattern of siloed development that would persist for decades

---

## 2010-2015: First Major Programs

### 2012
- **Event:** Launch of the Phoenix project concept
- **Details:** Ambitious plan to create a unified case management system for all courts
- **Significance:** Represented the first attempt at comprehensive digitalization

### 2014
- **Event:** Introduction of e-Deposit for Brussels courts
- **Details:** Electronic filing system allowing lawyers to submit documents digitally
- **Significance:** First functional digital tool, but limited in scope and adoption
- **Source:** [To be documented]

## 2016-2020: Renewed Efforts and Setbacks

### 2016
- **Event:** Minister Koen Geens announces "Digital Justice" plan
- **Details:** Multi-year roadmap for comprehensive digitalization
- **Significance:** Political commitment at ministerial level
- **Source:** [To be documented]

### 2018
- **Event:** Court of Audit report on justice IT spending
- **Details:** Critical assessment of fragmented spending and lack of results

### 2020
- **Event:** COVID-19 pandemic exposes digital gaps
