**Objective:** Obtain official recognition and authorization to conduct independent investigations into Belgian government failures.

**Why This Matters:**
- Legitimizes DOGE Europe's investigative work

- Signals institutional accountability commitment

**Success Metrics:**
- [ ] Parliamentary recognition of DOGE Europe investigation authority

- Demonstrate investigative rigor through Brussels case
- Build public pressure via transparency tools (Coalition Builder)

**Obstacles:**
- Political resistance from implicated parties
- Institutional inertia ("who are you to investigate us?")
- Legal barriers to document access
- Media capture by political interests

**Countermeasures:**
- Public transparency: publish all findings openly
- Citizen engagement: build grassroots support
- Legal strategy: use existing FOI laws aggressively
- International precedent: reference successful DOGE-style initiatives elsewhere

## Risk Assessment

### High Risks
- **Political retaliation:** Smear campaigns, legal harassment
- **Media blackout:** Findings ignored by mainstream press
- **Funding failure:** Citizens don't donate, project stalls
- **Legal challenges:** Lawsuits, gag orders

### Mitigation Strategies
- **Legal protection:** Partner with press freedom organizations
- **Decentralized publishing:** Use international platforms, blockchain for document security
- **Build alternative media:** Direct citizen communication via newsletter, social media
- **Financial reserves:** Transparent budget, minimal burn rate
